# 1️⃣ Which Organizations to Target (For MVP)

RBI regulates many entities, but most fintechs fall into **3 big buckets**.

These three will cover **80% of modern financial startups**.

---

## 1️⃣ Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs)

Most **lending startups** are NBFCs.

Examples:

* Lending apps
* Microfinance companies
* Vehicle loans
* BNPL platforms

Important regulations they follow include **Know Your Customer (KYC)** and other NBFC directions issued by the Reserve Bank of India.

Typical compliance areas:

* KYC compliance
* Digital lending guidelines
* Interest rate disclosure
* AML checks

Why choose NBFC:

✔ Huge number of companies
✔ Many regulatory changes
✔ Judges understand the use case

---

## 2️⃣ Payment Aggregators / Fintech Payment Platforms

Examples:

* Razorpay
* PayU
* Cashfree
* Payment gateways

They must follow RBI rules around:

* Payment security
* Data localization
* merchant onboarding
* fraud detection

These are governed by RBI’s Payment Aggregator frameworks.

Why choose:

✔ Highly relevant for fintech
✔ Strong compliance requirements

---

## 3️⃣ Commercial Banks

These are traditional banks regulated under RBI’s commercial banking directions.

Key compliance areas:

* Risk management
* capital adequacy
* climate risk reporting
* outsourcing risk
* internal ombudsman rules

Why include them:

✔ Most RBI circulars target banks
✔ Demonstrates broad applicability

# 4️⃣ Handling New RBI Regulations

This is the **key part of your hackathon solution**.

When RBI publishes a new circular:

Step 1

Scrape RBI website.

Step 2

Download PDF.

Step 3

Convert PDF → structured text.

Step 4

Extract key metadata.

Example JSON:

```json
{
  "title": "KYC Directions 2025",
  "category": "Commercial Banks",
  "compliance_area": "KYC",
  "summary": "Updated guidelines for customer verification",
  "effective_date": "2026-01-01",
  "impact_entities": ["Banks", "NBFC"]
}
```

# 5️⃣ Should You Merge New Embeddings?

This is a **very important design decision**.

You have two options.

---

## Option A (Bad)

Merge everything into **one vector database**.

Problem:

* Hard to track changes
* Hard to identify **latest rule**

---

## Option B (Better)

Use **separate collections**.

Example:

```
VectorDB
│
├── master_directions
│
├── circular_updates
│
└── compliance_cases
```

When query happens:

```
Search master_directions
+
Search circular_updates
```

Then combine results.
